An evolution of the Intermediaries legacy programme, Fak’ugesiPRO is the Festival’s dedicated talks and industry platform, curated for organisations and practitioners operating at the intersection of creativity, technology, research, and policy.

What is Fak'ugesiPRO

View 2026 programme

Studios, cultural institutions, production houses, government agencies, development finance organisations, tertiary institutions, and technology companies gather to address the practical realities shaping Africa’s digital ecosystems. Sessions span intellectual property, investment access, platform regulation, creative infrastructure, skills pipelines, and cross-border collaboration. Delegates gain access to facilitated introductions, closed roundtables, and strategic conversations linked to the broader Festival community and networks.

Join our industry
network

Fak’ugesiPRO is not a conference. It is where organisations that have decided to engage seriously with African digital creative infrastructure come to do the work of engagement: identifying partners, structuring agreements, and building lasting relationships. Registration is through the Visit page. Session numbers are limited. Early registration is advised.

Tshimologong Digital Innovation Precinct is the home of Fak'ugesi and a hub for Africa's digital creative and technology ecosystem.

The University of the Witwatersrand provides the academic and research infrastructure underpinning the Fak'ugesi festival and its programmes.

The Wits Anglo American Digital Dome is Africa's premier immersive environment, hosting the Immersive Africa strand of the festival.
