Theme: African Imaginaries

An Imaginary, in this context, is not a fantasy or a wishful projection. It is a framework: a structured set of conditions under which alternative futures become real. African Imaginaries spotlights the intellectual and creative work currently happening across the continent.

Practitioners in Nairobi, Lagos, Accra, Johannesburg, and Kigali are not waiting to see what the future looks like. They are designing it. Prototyping it. Archiving it. Building the infrastructure to scale. Fak'ugesi is where that work is assembled and showcased.

The theme organises across three frequencies:

  • Memory Engines — how African communities preserve, classify, and transmit heritage for the future.
  • Imagination Engines — the relationship between machine intelligence and African creative agency.
  • Future Engines — urban, climatic, and policy systems that African practitioners are building in real time.

Our Main Partners

Tshimologong Digital Innovation Precinct is the home of Fak'ugesi and a hub for Africa's digital creative and technology ecosystem.

The University of the Witwatersrand provides the academic and research infrastructure underpinning the Fak'ugesi festival and its programmes.

The Wits Anglo American Digital Dome is Africa's premier immersive environment, hosting the Immersive Africa strand of the festival.

The Origins Centre at Wits University is a world-class museum exploring the origins of humankind and southern African rock art heritage.
