Fak'ugesi Awards

Celebrating The Best
in African Digital Creativity

The Fak'ugesi Awards are the premier recognition of digital creative excellence across the African continent, presented annually as a highlight of the Fak'ugesi African Digital Innovation Festival. Founded in 2014 as a collaboration between the Tshimologong Precinct and the Wits School of Arts Digital Arts Department, the Awards honour outstanding creative work in immersive media, video games, animation, digital art, design, and music.

Requirements

  1. 1. Applicants must be 18 or older and reside in any African country. Submitted work must be the applicant's own original creation.
  2. 2. Applications must be submitted in one category only. Prototypes and early-stage work are eligible alongside completed projects. The core criterion is a compelling minimum viable product.
  3. 3. Work involving jointly owned intellectual property must include written consent from all rights holders. Self-nominations and third-party nominations are both accepted.
  4. 4. Nominations open August 2026. The adjudication panel is drawn from creative technology, academia, design, media, and industry leadership. Panel members are announced when nominations open.

Our Awards Partners

Tshimologong Digital Innovation Precinct is the home of Fak'ugesi and a hub for Africa's digital creative and technology ecosystem.

The University of the Witwatersrand provides the academic and research infrastructure underpinning the Fak'ugesi festival and its programmes.

The Wits Anglo American Digital Dome is Africa's premier immersive environment, hosting the Immersive Africa strand of the festival.

The Origins Centre at Wits University is a world-class museum exploring the origins of humankind and southern African rock art heritage.
