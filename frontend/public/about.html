Who we are

Fak'ugesi African Digital Innovation Festival is a cross-disciplinary innovation platform operating at the intersection of African imagination and systemic technological practice. Founded in 2014 at the Tshimologong Digital Innovation Precinct, Wits University, Johannesburg, it has run for twelve consecutive editions, each one a public test of what African digital practice can produce and where it can go.


The festival convenes practitioners who build without waiting for permission or precedent. Creatives, coders, archivists, urban planners, climate technologists, sound artists, researchers, and policy professionals gather to build, test, exhibit, and release work that matters well beyond the five days of the festival. Fak'ugesi does not describe the African digital future. It is a working environment in which that future is actively constructed.

Tshimologong Digital Innovation Precinct is the home of Fak'ugesi and a hub for Africa's digital creative and technology ecosystem.

The University of the Witwatersrand provides the academic and research infrastructure underpinning the Fak'ugesi festival and its programmes.

The Wits Anglo American Digital Dome is Africa's premier immersive environment, hosting the Immersive Africa strand of the festival.
