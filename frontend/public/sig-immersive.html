AfriVerse

AfriVerse is Fak’ugesi’s signature fulldome immersive programme, anchored by the Wits Anglo American Digital Dome — an 8K projection environment in which the distinction between image and space dissolves entirely. In October, the Dome becomes a portal into speculative futures, collective memory, and African imagination at scale.

FAQ

Everything you need to know about AfriVerse and the Wits Anglo American Digital Dome programme.

Submit your work →
Fulldome projection is an immersive projection format designed for dome-shaped architecture. A fulldome environment surrounds the viewer 360 degrees. The Wits Anglo American Digital Dome uses an 8K projection system, making it the most technically advanced public storytelling infrastructure on the African continent.
The expo is where work is encountered: displayed and engaged with at the viewer's own pace. The showcase is a structured programme in which work is experienced sequentially, curated, timed, and fully immersive. Some AfriVerse works sit in one category; others bridge both. The programme will indicate which format applies.
Commissioning and selection follows a curated process. Both original works and adaptations of existing work are considered. Proposals from African creatives working in fulldome, XR, and spatial sound are welcomed through the Get Involved section.
EUNIC is the European Network of National Cultural Institutes. AfriVerse is part of a broader Immersive Africa collaboration with the EUNIC network, running through to August 2026.
