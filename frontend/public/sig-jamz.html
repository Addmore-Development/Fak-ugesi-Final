About the Programme

What is Jamz?

Fak'ugesi Jamz is the festival's high-pressure, rapid-prototyping environment — a space where makers stop talking about ideas and start building them. Structured around tight time constraints and open provocations, Jamz brings together practitioners from animation, game development, design, and digital art to collaborate under real creative pressure.

Participants work in teams responding to provocations drawn from the festival's three Frequencies: Memory Engines, Imagination Engines, and Future Engines. The format is deliberately unpolished — what matters is the thinking, the experimentation, and the willingness to iterate fast and fail forward. Each strand runs in a structured heats format, with teams progressing through rounds to a final showcase.

Jamz is open to practitioners at all career stages. Whether you are a student making your first game, a seasoned animator looking for a creative challenge, or a developer who has never worked in a festival context before — the only requirement is genuine commitment to the process and a readiness to collaborate with people you may have just met.

Our Jamz Partners

Tshimologong Digital Innovation Precinct is the home of Fak'ugesi and a hub for Africa's digital creative and technology ecosystem.

The University of the Witwatersrand provides the academic and research infrastructure underpinning the Fak'ugesi festival and its programmes.

The Wits Anglo American Digital Dome is Africa's premier immersive environment, hosting the Immersive Africa strand of the festival.

The Origins Centre at Wits University is a world-class museum exploring the origins of humankind and southern African rock art heritage.
